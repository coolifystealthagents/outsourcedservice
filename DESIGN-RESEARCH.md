# Design research: OutsourcedService.com

## Search intent

Query: `outsourced services Philippines staffing`

The buyer is not looking for a giant catalog. They need to decide which recurring work belongs with a Filipino service team, then set the handoff, access, and review rules before hiring.

## Five direct organic competitors

### 1. Outsourced.ph

- URL: https://outsourced.ph/
- Strong points: dark navy hero, bright cyan accents, clear Philippines language, a useful role directory, and obvious calls to action.
- Weak points in the inspected desktop capture: a cookie panel covered much of the first screen; mixed illustration and photography felt uneven; several carousels clipped at the edge; the page ran long.

### 2. Sourcefit

- URL: https://sourcefit.com/
- Strong points: bold orange display type, facility video, direct CTA hierarchy, and concrete operating information.
- Weak points in the inspected desktop capture: the hero was dense, navigation was crowded, and unusually large blank areas appeared between sections. Sourcefit also promotes multiple sourcing regions, which does not fit this site's Philippines-only model.

### 3. Booth

- URL: https://hirebooth.com/
- Strong points: deep green hero, friendly oversized type, rounded photography, modular cards, and a people-first tone.
- Weak points in the inspected desktop capture: a report overlay blocked the hero, some sections appeared unloaded, and the page was too long. Booth's multi-country model is not suitable for this offer.

### 4. Staff Domain

- URL: https://www.staffdomain.com/
- Strong points: a memorable cobalt and lime palette, one sharp promise, restrained abstract graphics, early operational detail, and clean comparison modules.
- Weak points in the inspected desktop capture: the hero left too much empty space on the right, navigation felt crowded, and large blank gaps weakened the page.

### 5. Penbrothers

- URL: https://penbrothers.com/
- Strong points: direct Philippines language, heavy geometric type, bold blue fields, role discovery, and practical process content.
- Weak points in the inspected desktop capture: the hero lacked a visible CTA, a cookie panel interrupted the opening, gray cards appeared unfinished, and repeated oversized sections made the page feel longer than necessary.

## Chosen direction

Staff Domain is the primary visual reference. Its bold color blocking and plain operating language make the service feel easier to understand. The redesign keeps that confidence, then fixes the empty hero by adding an original service-workflow illustration. Penbrothers influenced the direct Philippines language. Booth influenced the softer card geometry and human scale.

## Original implementation

- Marker: `filipino-service-desk-2026-07`
- H1: `Build a Filipino service team around the work, not the job title.`
- Palette: ink blue, electric cobalt, lime, warm paper, and coral.
- Composition: compact header; asymmetric hero with a local workflow illustration; a handoff strip; four service lanes; an operating-rules section; guide cards; final intake panel.
- Logo: original OS monogram built from two linked service lanes. Local file: `/public/logo.svg`.
- Hero artwork: original SVG of two specialists reviewing a workflow board. Local file: `/public/filipino-service-workflow.svg`.
- Image semantics: the illustration shows people and a workflow board. It does not claim that appearance proves nationality. Nearby copy states that staffing is limited to Filipino talent.

## Guardrails

No competitor copy, logos, screenshots, or assets are used. The page contains no public rates, testimonials, ratings, client logos, awards, or unsupported performance claims. Every staffing reference limits talent sourcing to the Philippines. The site keeps its existing routes, legal pages, footer links, metadata, schema, copyright, and intake flow. The routing disclosure makes clear that this is an independent site and requests may go to a Philippines staffing partner.
